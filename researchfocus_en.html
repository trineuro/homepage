Research Focus


ADHD 개요

Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is one of the most common psychiatric disorders, affecting 2.2–7.2% of children and adolescents, and 2.8–5.0% of adults globally. The condition is characterized by persistent inattention, hyperactivity, and/or impulsive behavior that is more severe and more frequent than that typically observed in individuals of a similar developmental stage. This results in functional impairment in areas such as cognition, social interaction, employment, self-care, and mobility. The disorder is often diagnosed in early childhood, although recent studies have highlighted the potential for adult-onset ADHD. Due to its high prevalence, strong correlation with subsequent psychiatric co-morbidities, persistence into adulthood, and functional impairment across the life course, ADHD is now being recognized as an important disorder worldwide ADHD is a heritable condition with a strong genetic component. Evidence also supports an increased risk of ADHD in offspring who are born prematurely, or at low birth weight. ADHD is more common in boys compared with girls, although research indicates that the male predominance of ADHD in childhood may not persist into adulthood.
